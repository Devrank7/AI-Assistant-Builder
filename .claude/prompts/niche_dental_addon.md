### Pain points SPECIFIC to dental clinics:

- **Missed emergency calls**: Toothaches happen at night. Patient calls 3 clinics — books with the first one that answers.
- **Insurance questions**: 40-60% of calls are JUST asking "do you accept my insurance?" — a receptionist spends 2+ hours/day on this.
- **No-shows**: 15-25% of dental appointments are no-shows. Automated reminders reduce this by 30-50%.
- **Price shoppers**: Patients call 5+ clinics comparing prices. If nobody answers at your clinic — they don't call back.
- **After-hours emergencies**: Dental emergencies are 24/7. Clinic is open 9-6. The gap = lost patients.
- **Language barrier**: In Dubai — patients speak English, Arabic, Hindi, Urdu, Russian. Receptionist speaks 2 languages max. AI speaks all.

- "Average dental clinic loses 20-35% of potential patients due to missed calls and slow response times"

### Dental-specific objection:

**"Patients need to talk to a real person for medical advice"**

```
Absolutely — and the assistant never gives medical advice. It says things like "I'd recommend scheduling a consultation with Dr. [name] so they can examine you properly. Would you like to book an appointment?"

It handles the booking logistics — time, insurance, location questions — and routes anything medical to your team immediately. Think of it as a smart receptionist, not a doctor.
```
