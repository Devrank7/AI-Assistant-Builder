### Pain points SPECIFIC to beauty salons:

- **Instagram is their LIFELINE**: 70-80% of new clients come through Instagram. But responding to 50+ DMs/day is impossible while working on clients.
- **"Left on read" problem**: Client sends DM at 8pm, salon responds next morning, client already booked elsewhere.
- **Price questions = 50% of DMs**: Half of all messages are just "how much is X?" — repetitive and time-consuming.
- **Last-minute cancellations**: 20-30% cancellation rate. Slots go empty = lost revenue.
- **Multi-stylist scheduling**: Client wants a specific stylist on a specific day — impossible to coordinate via DM.
- **Weekend rush**: Friday-Saturday (UAE) is peak. Everyone messages Thursday night to book. Staff is overwhelmed.
- **Portfolio requests**: "Can you show me examples of balayage?" — sending photos manually takes time.

### Beauty-specific objection:

**"My clients want a personal touch — AI is too impersonal"**

```
Totally get it — beauty is personal. And the assistant doesn't replace that personal touch at all. What it does is handle the "Can I book Saturday?" and "How much is highlights?" messages at 10pm when you and your team are at home.

Think about it — is it more personal to respond instantly at 10pm with "Hi! Highlights start at AED 400, and we have Saturday availability at 2pm and 4pm. Would you like to book?" — or to leave them waiting 12 hours until morning?

Your personal touch is in the salon. The assistant just makes sure they GET to the salon.
```

### Beauty-specific objection:

**"I already use Fresha/Booksy/Vagaro for bookings"**

```
Great — those are solid booking platforms. The assistant actually works WITH them, not instead of them.

Here's the gap: Fresha handles bookings, but it doesn't answer questions. When someone DMs you "do you do balayage on short hair?" — Fresha can't help with that. The assistant answers the question AND then sends them to your Fresha link to book.

It's the conversation layer that sits on top of your booking system.
```
