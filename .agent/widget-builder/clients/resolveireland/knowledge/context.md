Independent Investigations What are they and when are they invoked? A formal independent process, set up in response to a complaint or a concern identified. Usually conducted in line with the organisation&rsquo;s relevant policy. Allows all aspects of a complaint or concern to be considered.&nbsp; They are conducted independently of any disciplinary hearing process Conclusions reached are noted in a report at the conclusion of the investigation, with recommendations included if appropriate Workplace Investigations are set up in response to complaints or concerns arising from a dispute between colleagues or between employees and their employers.&nbsp; Investigations are often conducted into complaints of alleged bullying and/or harassment, disciplinary issues and other concerns identified.&nbsp; Also, can be used in the form of a management investigation where an issue exists which is affecting the wellbeing and performance of a team &nbsp; Benefits of using our Independent Investigation services Ensures that the facts are established in an objective and impartial process Transparency and openness throughout the whole process Clear separation of facts and recommendations from judgements Team of credible, competent &amp; highly experienced practitioners&nbsp; Extensive experience in conducting independent investigations with private and public sector organisations. ​ ​ Resolve Ireland &copy; 2024 Privacy Statement bottom of page

Protected Disclosures Ireland&rsquo;s whistleblowing legislation, the Protected Disclosures Act 2014, commenced on 15 July 2014. The legislation set a new standard of international best practice for whistleblowing in Ireland. &nbsp;The full implementation into Irish law of the European Union directive (2019/1937) was carried out in 2022 with a commencement date of January 1st, 2023. This resulted in a range of enhanced protections for workers under the act, a widening of the areas of potential wrongdoing and a number of offences introduced for organisations or individuals who do not comply with the provisions of the legislation.&nbsp; &nbsp; Public Sector organisations and private sector organisation with more than 250 employees are already required to meet the obligations set out in the new legislation. From December 17th2023, organisations with 50 employees or more are required to establish formal channels and procedures for their employees or workers with their organisations to make protected disclosures. &nbsp; Resolve Ireland have been providing external independent confidential disclosure services in relation to this legislation since 2014. We work with organisations who wish to create and maintain a supportive environment within which their employees can raise issues of concern in an appropriate and effective way. Our clients include organisations in the private and public sector who are committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethics and accountability. Our services can ensure peace of mind, full compliance and reduce associated risks for employers and employees.
